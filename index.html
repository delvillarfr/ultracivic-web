Heal Earth.

The cause of the climate crisis is the fact that polluters do not care much if their emissions harm society. Our view is that emissions trading systems address this problem head on. In an emissions trading system, the government decides to cap the aggregate emissions (in tons of CO2) of a set of regulated firms and issues the corresponding amount of permits. It then requires the regulated firms to own a permit for the right to emit each ton of CO2. Firms can trade these permits, according to their needs and costs of reducing emissions. Naturally, those who find it hard to reduce emissions buy permits from those who find it easy. Later, the regulated firms meet the cap on aggregate emissions, with most of the reduction coming from firms who find it easy to curb their emissions. In an ideal configuration, the supply of permits induces an equilibrium permit price equal to the social cost of carbon — the cost to society of releasing an additional ton of CO2 into the atmosphere. Here, polluters effectively consider the social harm of their emissions when they make production decisions.

When you finance the removal of a carbon permit, you perfect the emissions trading system and make polluters care about the social costs of their emissions.
